Advanced Technology

Modern Tools for
Clearer Diagnosis & Comfort

We invest in up-to-date imaging and digital workflows so your visits are efficient, comfortable, and easy to understand — with less radiation where it counts and fewer surprises when we plan your care.

📷
Imaging

Digital X-rays

High-resolution digital radiographs develop instantly on-screen, so we can zoom, enhance contrast, and explain what we see together. Digital sensors typically use less radiation than traditional film and eliminate chemical processing — better for you and the environment.

Ask about imaging at your visit →
🖥️
Impressions

Digital Dental Scanners

Intraoral scanners capture thousands of images of your teeth to build an accurate 3D model — often without messy impression material. Digital scans support crowns, bridges, clear aligners, and night guards with excellent fit and fewer remakes.

Common Questions

We've Got Answers

Curious about a treatment, costs, or what to expect? Here are the questions we hear most often — answered honestly and in plain English.

For most patients, we recommend a checkup and professional cleaning every six months. If you have a history of gum disease, frequent cavities, or other concerns, we may recommend more frequent visits. Regular appointments let us catch problems while they're small — before they become bigger and more expensive to fix.

Absolutely. Our digital X-rays emit a tiny fraction of the radiation of traditional film X-rays — a single bitewing exposes you to just 0.001 millisieverts, about 1/2,800th of a CT scan. We also only take X-rays when they're clinically necessary. The diagnostic information they provide far outweighs any minimal risk.

Modern root canal therapy has gotten a bad reputation it doesn't deserve. With today's anesthetics and techniques, most patients tell us it feels no different from getting a filling. In fact, a root canal relieves the throbbing pain of a tooth infection — most people feel dramatically better immediately after the procedure. We also offer nitrous oxide for extra comfort.

The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends a child's first dental visit by their first birthday, or when their first tooth appears — whichever comes first. Early visits help us catch developmental issues, establish healthy habits, and most importantly, get your child comfortable with the dental environment before any anxiety sets in.

With proper care — brushing, flossing, and regular checkups — the titanium implant post can last a lifetime, as it fuses permanently with your jawbone. The crown on top typically lasts 15–25 years before it may need replacement. Implants are widely considered the most durable and natural-feeling tooth replacement option available.

Veneers are thin porcelain shells that cover only the front surface of a tooth — primarily cosmetic. Crowns cover the entire tooth and are used when a tooth is significantly damaged, decayed, or has had a root canal. Think of veneers as a cosmetic enhancement and crowns as a restorative solution — though both can beautifully improve your smile's appearance.

Bleeding gums are usually a sign of gingivitis — early-stage gum disease caused by plaque buildup along the gumline. The good news: gingivitis is completely reversible with a professional cleaning and improved home care. Please don't ignore it — left untreated, it progresses to periodontitis, which can lead to bone and tooth loss. Give us a call.

Call us immediately for: severe or sudden tooth pain, a knocked-out or loosened permanent tooth, a cracked or broken tooth, a lost crown or filling, jaw or facial swelling, signs of abscess (fever, foul taste, swelling), or uncontrolled oral bleeding. Our simple rule: if you're asking yourself “is this an emergency?” — it probably is. Call (516) 485-9675 and we’ll guide you.

Professional in-office whitening delivers the fastest and most dramatic results — brightening by up to 8 shades in about an hour. Custom take-home whitening trays (made from impressions of your teeth) are also highly effective and let you whiten at your own pace. Over-the-counter strips can help with maintenance but can't match professional-grade results.

Yes. Ultimate Smile Dental offers sedation dentistry to help anxious patients relax — part of our commitment to pleasant, comfortable visits. Tell us how you’re feeling when you call or at your appointment so we can discuss the best approach for you.
